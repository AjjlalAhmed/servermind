What can you do with ServerMind?

ServerMind is a self-hosted AI assistant for your Linux servers — you chat with your box (or a whole fleet) in plain English and it runs the right read-only checks, shows a live dashboard, and makes changes only when you arm it. Here's who reaches for it and the jobs it does best.

Indie developers & solo founders

You shipped the app; now one VPS quietly runs it. Manage the box without becoming a sysadmin — ask instead of remembering commands.

Is the site healthy? Why is it slow?

Agencies & freelancers

A dozen client servers, all different. Put them under one login as a fleet, get one alerts inbox, and ask "which servers are unhealthy?"

Which servers are unhealthy?

Startups without DevOps

No dedicated SRE yet. Give the whole team safe, plain-English access to production — guardrails stop anyone (or the AI) breaking it.

On-call & incident response

Pager goes off at 2am. Skip the syntax — ask what changed, read the logs, find the memory hog, and fix it once you arm the change.

Why is nginx down?

Homelabs & self-hosting

Docker, Pi-hole, media servers, the works. A private AI copilot for your stack that needs no domain and can run fully offline on Ollama.

Are all my containers up?

Safe database operations

Ask "how many active orders?" and get the number — the AI writes a read-only SELECT that's validated before it runs. No write access, ever.

How many signups today?

One tool, many jobs

However you use it, the shape is the same: ServerMind is self-hosted (no cloud backend, no telemetry), it talks to your server through a read-only command allowlist so there's no raw shell to exploit, and any change — a restart, a stop — is refused until you flip a server-side arm switch the AI can't touch. You bring your own AI: the free Google Gemini tier, a Claude subscription, or any OpenAI-compatible API including local Ollama for zero data egress.
